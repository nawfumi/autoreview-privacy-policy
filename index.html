Privacy Policy

AutoReview automates repetitive on-screen questions — star ratings, Yes/No prompts — using Android's Accessibility Service. This page explains exactly why that permission is needed, what the service does, and what happens (and doesn't happen) to your data.

01Why AutoReview needs Accessibility Service

Android's Accessibility Service API is the only system-level API that lets an app read the content of another app's screen and act on it — the same capability that powers screen readers and switch-access tools. AutoReview uses it to detect a question on screen and tap the answer you've already told it to give.

AutoReview
Accessibility Service · currently on

This is the exact toggle you control in Settings → Accessibility → AutoReview. Turning it off stops all screen-reading and tapping immediately.

Without this permission, AutoReview would need you to manually answer every single question every time — defeating the entire purpose of the app.

02What the service actually does

  • Watches for screen content changes only while the service is enabled and only within the app you're using it against.
  • Matches visible question text against the presets you've defined yourself.
  • Taps the star rating or Yes/No answer that corresponds to a matched question.
  • Surfaces any question it doesn't recognize in an "Unrecognized Question" screen so you can map it once, manually.

AutoReview does not record your screen, take screenshots, log keystrokes outside of matching your saved presets, or read/interact with any app other than the one you've pointed it at.

03What data is collected and stored

Your question presets — the questions you define and the answers you assign to them — are stored locally on your device only, using Android DataStore with a Protobuf-backed schema.

Unrecognized question text is held only long enough for you to map it inside the app, then saved locally alongside your other presets. It is never transmitted anywhere.

No backend for core functionality. AutoReview has no server of its own — your presets and question mappings are never transmitted anywhere. The only outside data flow in the app is the advertising SDK described below.

05Data sharing

AutoReview does not share, sell, or transmit any data to third parties — because no data leaves your device in the first place.

06Advertising

AutoReview shows ads through Unity LevelPlay, a mediation platform that runs an auction across multiple ad networks (including Unity Ads and, where connected, partners such as AdMob or Meta Audience Network) to serve each ad. This is separate from the on-device automation described above and is the only component of the app that communicates with an external service.

To serve and measure ads, LevelPlay and its connected ad networks may collect information such as device identifiers, approximate location (derived from IP), app usage and ad interaction data, in line with standard mobile advertising practices. This data is collected and processed by Unity and its mediated ad networks directly — it does not pass through AutoReview's own storage and is unrelated to your question presets.

Unity LevelPlay
Ad mediation · governed by Unity's privacy policy

Full details on what Unity and its mediated networks collect: unity.com/legal/privacy-policy

We recommend reviewing Unity's privacy policy directly, since LevelPlay's mediated networks and their individual data practices can change independently of this app.

07Your control

You can disable the Accessibility Service for AutoReview at any time via Settings → Accessibility → AutoReview. Doing so immediately stops all screen-reading and auto-tap behavior. Uninstalling the app removes all locally stored presets along with it.
